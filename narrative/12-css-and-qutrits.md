# CSS codes from Barnes–Wall pairs, and the qutrit analogue

The grade-zero stratum of $L_3$ recovers the single-qubit Clifford group. The next step up is to build *stabilizer codes* out of the lattice. The Calderbank–Shor–Steane (CSS) construction takes a pair of classical binary codes satisfying a duality inclusion and produces a quantum code; specialising to Reed–Muller pairs gives an infinite family of Barnes–Wall–shaped CSS codes whose grade enumerator is the same $8 \cdot 4^m \cdot (1 + X)^m$ closed form derived combinatorially in chapter 06. The qutrit analogue replaces $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_8]$ with $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_9]$ and asks which of the qubit-level grade identities survive. The answer is one substantive headline at $d = 3$: most of the closed-form structure does survive, and the cT/CCZ grade-vs-nullity incomparability is preserved.

## The Reed–Muller CSS family

A binary Reed–Muller code $\mathrm{RM}(r, m)$ has block length $n = 2^m$, dimension $\binom{m}{\le r} = \sum_{i \le r} \binom{m}{i}$, and minimum distance $2^{m - r}$. The duality identity is

```math
\mathrm{RM}(r, m)^\perp \;=\; \mathrm{RM}(m - r - 1, m).
```
For any pair $r_2 < r_1$ with $r_1 + r_2 \le m - 1$, the inclusion $\mathrm{RM}(r_2, m) \subseteq \mathrm{RM}(r_1, m)^\perp$ holds; this is the CSS condition. The CSS code from this pair is

```math
\mathrm{BWCss}(m, r_1, r_2) \;:=\; \mathrm{CSS}\bigl(\mathrm{RM}(r_2, m), \mathrm{RM}(r_1, m)\bigr),
```
with parameters

```math
[[n = 2^m,\ k = \tbinom{m}{\le r_1} - \tbinom{m}{\le r_2},\ d \ge 2^{m - r_1}]].
```
Canonical instances drop out of small parameter choices:

| $(m, r_1, r_2)$ | parameters | name |
|---|---|---|
| $(3, 1, 0)$ | $[[8, 3, 4]]$ | extended Hamming-CSS (parent of Steane $[[7, 1, 3]]$ by one puncture) |
| $(4, 2, 0)$ | $[[16, 10, 4]]$ | standard $[[16, 10, 4]]$ |
| $(4, 2, 1)$ | $[[16, 6, 4]]$ | stretched form |
| $(5, 2, 1)$ | $[[32, 10, 8]]$ | level-5 stretch |

The distance value is a lower bound on the $X$-distance; the full CSS distance can be larger.

## The grade enumerator closed form

For a fixed level $m$, the grade-refined enumerator of the family — counting logical-$X$ coset representatives by their Barnes–Wall grade rather than their Hamming weight — admits the same factorised form as the combinatorial enumerator of chapter 06:

```math
W_{\mathrm{BWCss}(m, r_1, r_2)}^{\mathrm{grade}}(X) \;=\; 8 \cdot 4^m \cdot (1 + X)^m.
```
The remarkable thing is that two independent paths produce the same expression. One path (chapter 06) is the $\lambda$-adic arithmetic of $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_8]$, counting phase polynomials of degree at most one by their grade. The other path (this chapter) is the binary-linear-algebra side: enumerating $\mathbb{F}_2$-linear coset representatives of $\mathrm{RM}(r_2, m)$ inside $\mathrm{RM}(r_1, m)$, with the BW-grade weighting on each representative pulled across the natural identification of the logical-$X$ coset space with the grade-1 stratum.

The Lean module [`../lean/StabilizerBW/BWCss/Grade.lean`](../lean/StabilizerBW/BWCss/Grade.lean) closes the closed-form identity unconditionally on the *grade enumerator* of phase polynomials of degree at most one. The corresponding *logical-operator* enumerator of $\mathrm{BWCss}(m, r_1, r_2)$ matches this expression provided one accepts the standard identification of the CSS logical-$X$ coset structure with the grade-1 phase-polynomial stratum, carried in that file as a named hypothesis. The substantive convergence is that two structurally different objects — phase polynomials and logical operators — produce the same closed form, suggesting a common combinatorial skeleton beneath the algebra. The expected refinement, queued for later development, is to bridge the carried hypothesis by an explicit map and so close the identification unconditionally.

## The qutrit analogue

Climbing sideways from $d = 2$ to $d = 3$, the cyclotomic ring changes from $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_8]$ to $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_9]$. The qutrit Barnes–Wall lattice $L_3^{(3)} \subset \mathbb{Z}[\zeta_9]^3$ replaces the standard pair construction with a triple. The qutrit Clifford group is generated by the qutrit $S$, $H$, and CNOT (with the obvious dimension changes), and the qutrit $T$ gate is the qudit eighth-of-the-way phase

```math
T^{(3)} \;=\; \mathrm{diag}(1, \zeta_9, \zeta_9).
```
The grade at $d = 3$ uses the totally ramified prime $\lambda^{(3)} = 1 - \zeta_9$ above the rational prime $3$, with $v_{\lambda^{(3)}}(3) = 6$ (the ramification index of $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_9]$ over $\mathbb{Q}$ is $6$, not $2$ as at $d = 2$).

The substantive headline at $d = 3$, kernel-checked in [`../lean/StabilizerBW/Qutrit/CSSBarnesWall/FullyGeneralHeadline.lean`](../lean/StabilizerBW/Qutrit/CSSBarnesWall/FullyGeneralHeadline.lean), is the *fully general qutrit arithmetic-view headline*:

> The qutrit Barnes–Wall lattice over $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_9]$ supports a CSS-style code construction, with the Möbius/finite-difference closed form for grades on diagonal phase polynomials carrying over verbatim from $d = 2$, the qutrit T-gate sitting at grade $1$ in $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_9]$, and the cT/CCZ grade-vs-stabilizer-nullity incomparability holding with the same witnesses as at $d = 2$.

In words: the *form* of the closed-form theorems is dimension-stable from $d = 2$ to $d = 3$, even though the underlying ring changes ramification index. The specific cardinalities and grade values change, but the structural identities (Möbius closed form, grade-equals-one-for-T, incomparability with nullity) are preserved.

## What does not carry from $d = 2$

Two facts at $d = 2$ do not carry, and pinning them is part of the honest scope.

First, the level-3 single-qubit lattice $L_3$ has the elegant pair description $(1 + i) \mid a + b$. The level-3 single-qutrit lattice $L_3^{(3)}$ has a more involved triple description involving a sum constraint at a non-trivial sublattice index, and there is no single one-line description analogous to $(1 + i) \mid a + b$ at $d = 3$. The constructive description is by direct generators in $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_9]^3$.

Second, the unitary automorphism converse $\mathrm{Aut}(L_3) \cap U(2) = \{\pm \zeta_8^k\} \cdot \mathrm{Clifford}_1$ has no kernel-checked $d = 3$ analogue. The qutrit Clifford group is larger and the enumeration argument that closes the $d = 2$ case at a $16$-element search space does not generalise cleanly to $d = 3$.

## The Eisenstein toy model

The Eisenstein integers $\mathbb{Z}[\omega]$ with $\omega = e^{2\pi i / 3}$ form an alternative cyclotomic ring at $d = 3$, distinct from $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_9]$ (which is the full ninth-roots-of-unity ring). The Eisenstein-ring construction in [`../lean/StabilizerBW/Qutrit/EisensteinToy/`](../lean/StabilizerBW/Qutrit/EisensteinToy/) is a *test model*: it tests which qubit-level grade computations are intrinsic to the structure of the lattice versus an artefact of the choice of cyclotomic level. Specific tests recorded in that subtree include the strict-subset bound at level 3, the Möbius identity on monomials of small support, and the qutrit phase-polynomial grade evaluation against a hand-computed table.

The Eisenstein toy model is not the qutrit Barnes–Wall theory. It is the smallest cyclotomic-ring-at-$d = 3$ that makes the grade machinery well-defined, and it is useful for sanity-checking the more substantial $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_9]$ development. The relationship is similar to the relationship between the level-2 BW lattice $L_2$ (the Gaussian-integer-level toy) and the level-3 BW lattice $L_3$ at $d = 2$: the toy is informative but does not contain the operator-theoretic content.

## What's new, what's borrowed

The CSS construction is Calderbank–Shor–Steane (1996, 1996, 1996 in three separate papers), and the Reed–Muller CSS family is implicit in the early CSS-code literature (Steane, *Multiple particle interference and quantum error correction*, 1996). The specific identification of $\mathrm{BWCss}(3, 1, 0)$ with the extended Hamming-CSS is folklore. What this repository adds, on the qubit side, is the grade-refined enumerator closed form $8 \cdot 4^m \cdot (1 + X)^m$ for the family, derived by two independent paths (combinatorial and CSS-coding-theoretic), and the named-hypothesis identification that links them.

On the qutrit side, the recasting of the qubit-level constructions in $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_9]$ — the CSS containment, the Möbius backbone, the T-gate grade at $d = 3$, the cT/CCZ incomparability — is, to the best of our knowledge, new at the kernel-checked level. The Eisenstein toy model is recorded explicitly as a test model, not as a competitor to the $\mathbb{Z}[\zeta_9]$ development.
